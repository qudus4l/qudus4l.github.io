Oak & D WhatsApp Shipping Assistant

Oak & D Canada May 2025 – Aug 2025

Project Overview

The Oak & D WhatsApp Shipping Assistant is a production-grade conversational logistics platform that lets shippers manage pricing quotes, supported routes, shipment tracking, drop-off logistics, package arrivals, complaints, and escalations directly inside WhatsApp. Instead of a static FAQ bot, it orchestrates live pricing tables, route definitions, service point data, and shipment events so every reply reflects the current operational state.

The solution is composed of a FastAPI webhook that drives WhatsApp conversations, a dedicated Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) microservice backed by OpenAI GPT-4.1-nano and ChromaDB, and a Flask dashboard for operations teams to manage pricing, routing, escalations, manual takeover, and observability. Each component talks to a shared SQLite (bot_dashboard.db) store, ensuring consistent data for automation and humans.

Core Customer Flows

  1. Pricing & Route Discovery: Parses origin, destination, shipping mode (air, sea, frozen), and weight, fetches live rates from the dashboard API, and keeps per-caller pricing context until the quote is complete.
  2. Shipment Tracking & Notifications: Resolves tracking IDs via ParcelTrackingAPI while also powering proactive bulk updates through `/api/package_status`, logging every status change for dashboard visibility.
  3. Service Points & Drop-Off Logic: Multi-intent detection handles “where do I drop it?” requests in parallel with pricing, fetches service point catalogs, and guides customers through pickup or partner agent options.
  4. Package Arrival Choices: `/api/package_arrival` fan-outs WhatsApp template notifications with interactive pickup vs delivery buttons and persists preferences for follow-up actions.
  5. Complaints & Escalations: Structured complaint flows capture issue type, tracking IDs, and summaries before opening escalation tickets and enabling manual agent takeover sessions when needed.

Challenges & Solutions

LLM-Driven Intent Recognition for Logistics

Oak & D’s customers mix pricing, routing, drop-off, and complaints in a single sentence, and keyword rules collapse under that ambiguity.

Solution: I built an OpenAI GPT-4.1-nano powered intent recognizer that emits structured JSON with multi-intent arrays plus normalized entities (origins, destinations, weights, shipping types, item classes). Specialized contexts like `_pricing_context`, `_service_point_context`, and `_complaint_context` maintain continuity between turns.

Stateful Pricing with Live Route Configs

Manual WhatsApp chats relied on spreadsheets and memory, creating inconsistency and errors whenever prices or city rules changed.

Solution: `_handle_pricing_intent` now resolves geographic ambiguity (“Canada” vs “Calgary”), queries the Flask dashboard’s `/api/get_prices`, and feeds a ResponseGenerator that explains base rates, minimum weights, clearance fees, and totals. Context storage lets customers add missing info later without restarting the flow.

Tracking Lifecycle & Package Arrivals

Operations needed both pull-based tracking answers and push-based arrival fan-outs with reliable history and WhatsApp template compliance.

Solution: I implemented `/api/package_status` and `/api/package_arrival` endpoints that normalize phone numbers, log every notification, and trigger template messages or interactive options (“Pick up” vs “Deliver”). Follow-up handlers interpret free text or button replies and keep package_arrivals plus package_status_history synchronized.

Human-in-the-Loop Escalations

Agents needed to step into conversations gracefully, view structured complaint data, and push manual replies without racing the bot.

Solution: A dedicated escalation pipeline stores ESC-* tickets with conversation history, while takeover endpoints (`/api/takeover/start|end|status`) pause automation per phone number. The dashboard can inject responses via `/api/update_history`, and every hand-off is logged for observability.

Results & Impact

Impact metrics are based on Oak & D’s internal analytics; update the exact numbers with the latest dashboard exports.

  • Handles 80–120 WhatsApp conversations per day across pricing, routing, tracking, and general inquiries with consistent SLA coverage.
  • Automates more than 70% of pricing and tracking questions end-to-end, letting the operations team focus on escalations and exceptions.
  • Reduces manual pricing lookup time by ~65% by enforcing a single source of truth through the dashboard APIs.
  • Maintains policy compliance by using WhatsApp templates for proactive notifications while falling back to session messages when allowed.
  • Delivers full observability via bot_dashboard.db so every interaction, escalation, and notification is reviewable in the Flask dashboard.

Key Learnings

  • Designing LLM-backed intent schemas for logistics workloads requires strict JSON contracts, entity normalization, and multi-intent orchestration.
  • Bridging operational systems (pricing tables, routing rules, clearance policies) into conversational UX demands deterministic business logic layered under the LLM.
  • Combining RAG with transactional flows keeps explanations grounded—policy snippets enrich replies without replacing domain logic.
  • Human-in-the-loop tooling (manual takeover, escalation tickets, dashboard injects) must be first-class to keep agents aligned with automation.
  • Shipping a “real” logistics assistant means wiring every WhatsApp turn to pricing APIs, tracking feeds, and arrival workflows rather than stopping at FAQ answers.
